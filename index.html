RM 04Ground · East

The Living Room

This room won't have many rules.

Some evenings we'll sit here after work.

Pretending we're about to watch a movie.

We won't.

We'll spend forty-five minutes deciding what to watch.

Then choose the thing we almost picked at the beginning.

Then talk through half of it anyway.

Somehow that will still count as quality time.

And years later,

we probably won't remember the movie.

Just who we watched it with.

RM 16First · Private

The Bedroom

· · ·

The funny thing about long distance is that it teaches you to dream about big moments.

Airports.

Train stations.

Reunions.

Countdowns.

For a long time, I thought those would be the moments I'd look forward to most.

But lately,

I think it'll be something much smaller.

Reaching for you.
And not having to check a screen first.

I think it'll be asking:

“Did you lock the door?”
and hearing
“Yes.”
from the other side of the room.

Not because it's a special moment.

Because it isn't.

Because it's ordinary.

And somehow,

that's exactly what makes it special.

Every time.
